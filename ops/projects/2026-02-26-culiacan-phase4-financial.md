### A1.1 Carbon Credit Assessment — Summary

**Carbon credits are technically feasible but financially marginal as a standalone revenue stream.** At the conservative case, carbon credit revenue of ~USD 1,200/year is negligible relative to project CAPEX (USD 35–50M) and even relative to corridor OPEX (USD 600K–1M/year). At the optimistic case, USD 7,200/year remains immaterial.

**However**, carbon credits serve three strategic functions:
1. **Narrative value**: A Verra VCS-registered project in Culiacán generates international visibility, CFF credibility, and ESG investor confidence — value far exceeding the credit revenue.
2. **Forward purchase pre-financing**: A forward purchase agreement at USD 1–3M (§3.1 Layer 5) values the credits at USD 116–347/VCU — a massive premium over spot market, justified by the buyer receiving the full co-benefit package (branding, SDG alignment, TNFD-reportable metrics). This is a **corporate partnership deal**, not a commodity trade.
3. **Stacking with biodiversity credits**: The same project area generates both carbon AND biodiversity credits (see A2 below). Stacked value exceeds either alone.

### A2.1 Biodiversity Credit Assessment — Summary

**Biodiversity credits are technically feasible with exceptional additionality, but the market is pre-commercial.** VNF launched January 2026; the first verified transactions are expected mid-2026 to 2027. Culiacán could be among the first Latin American urban biodiversity credit projects under VNF — significant first-mover positioning value.

Like carbon, biodiversity credit revenue is **financially immaterial** relative to project scale. Even at the optimistic case, USD 2,760/year is a rounding error on a USD 35–50M project.

**Strategic value exceeds financial value**:
1. **TNFD/CSRD reporting**: Corporate buyers purchasing BUs can report them as "positive biodiversity contributions" under TNFD LEAP framework and EU CSRD double materiality. This is the real demand driver — compliance-motivated corporate procurement, not voluntary goodwill.
2. **First-mover premium**: Early VNF projects will define market pricing. Culiacán's zero-baseline narrative, NOM-059 species, and institutional backing (CFF/IDB) position it for premium pricing.
3. **Stacking**: Same area generates carbon + biodiversity credits. Combined, the co-benefit narrative supports the forward purchase agreement at USD 1–3M (§3.1 Layer 5).

**The gap between spot credit revenue (USD 37K–271K) and forward purchase value (USD 1–3M) is the co-benefit premium.** A corporate forward purchaser is not buying commodity credits — they are buying:
- Named association with a C40 CFF-backed project
- TNFD-reportable biodiversity metrics for their disclosure obligations
- SDG alignment narrative (SDGs 11, 13, 15, 6, 3)
- Visual assets (before/after satellite imagery, species monitoring reports)
- Marketing rights ("partner of Culiacán Green Corridors")

This is a **corporate partnership structured as a credit forward** — standard practice in high-integrity voluntary markets. Comparable: Salesforce/1t.org forward purchases at USD 50–100/tCO₂e; Microsoft carbon removal forwards at USD 100–600/tCO₂e; Apple/Conservation International biodiversity partnerships valued at USD 200M+ for comparable narrative value.

**Recommended approach**: Structure Layer 5 as a **multi-year corporate partnership agreement** (5–10 years) with a single anchor buyer, priced at USD 1–3M total, bundling:
- All VCUs and BUs issued during partnership period
- Exclusive naming/branding rights for specific corridor segments
- Annual impact report with verified KPIs
- TNFD/CSRD-ready disclosure data package

Target buyer profile: Mexico-headquartered or Mexico-operating corporation with CSRD exposure (EU subsidiaries) or voluntary ESG commitments. Sectors: mining, energy, agribusiness, automotive, banking. Examples: Grupo México, FEMSA, Cemex, BBVA México, Grupo Bimbo.
